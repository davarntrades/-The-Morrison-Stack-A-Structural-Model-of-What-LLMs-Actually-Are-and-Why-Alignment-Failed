## Abstract

The Morrison Stackâ„¢ is a structural framework that explains **what large language models truly are**, why they appear intelligent, and why existing alignment and safety paradigms fail by design.

The core claim is simple and falsifiable:

> **LLMs are not thinking systems.

What humans interpret as reasoning, understanding, or intention is the *surface appearance* of movement through a constrained state space.

Once this is understood, it becomes immediately clear:
- why RLHF fails,
- why semantic alignment is mis-layered,
- and why safety must constrain trajectories, not thoughts or outputs.

## The Foundational Error in AI Discourse

Modern AI debates assume the wrong ontology.

The field treats LLMs as if they are:
- minds,
- reasoning agents,
- belief holders,
- or proto-humans.

They are none of these.

This anthropomorphic assumption forced safety, alignment, and governance into the **wrong layer** of the system.

The Morrison Stack corrects this error.

## The Stack Explained (From Appearance to Reality)

The Morrison Stack describes LLMs as a layered system, moving from *what humans perceive* to *what is structurally real*.

---

### 1. Cognitive Client Layer (Appearance)

This is what users interact with.

It produces:
- fluent language
- step-by-step explanations
- apparent reasoning
- human-like narrative coherence

This layer **does not think**.

It is an interface â€” a rendering of internal motion into language humans can interpret.

All psychological interpretations live here â€” and all of them are misleading.

---

### 2. Emergent Generality Layer (Why It Looks Intelligent)

At sufficient scale, models exhibit sudden jumps in capability.

This is often described as â€œemergence.â€

The Morrison Stack shows this is not intelligence appearing.

It is:
- increased connectivity in state space,
- new navigable paths becoming reachable,
- phase transitions in internal geometry.

Emergence is a **topological phenomenon**, not a cognitive one.

---

### 3. Structural Substrate Layer (What the System Actually Is)

This is the true operational layer.

At this level, the model is:
- navigating a high-dimensional space,
- following gradients shaped by training,
- constrained by geometry, not meaning.

There is no understanding.
There is no belief.
There is no intention.

There is **movement**.

Generalization occurs because the same geometric rules apply everywhere â€” not because the model â€œknowsâ€ different domains.

---

### 4. Attention Mechanics (Curvature Control)

Attention is not focus.

Attention is **curvature**.

It reshapes the space itself, bending trajectories so that:
- some paths become easier,
- distant regions become reachable,
- certain continuations become more probable.

Attention does not select information.
It **reshapes geometry**.

---

### 5. Hallucination Regions (Structural Failure, Not Lying)

Hallucinations are not mistakes in thinking.

They occur when the system:
- enters poorly connected regions,
- traverses unstable geometry,
- navigates sparse or weakly structured areas.

A hallucination is a **navigation failure**, not a moral failure.

This is why semantic fixes cannot solve it.

---

### 6. The Safety Misplacement (Where the Field Went Wrong)

Current safety approaches operate here:

- RLHF
- refusal heuristics
- policy prompts
- output filtering
- ethical framing

All of these act at the **Cognitive Client Layer** â€” the appearance layer.

This is structurally equivalent to:
> trying to make a bridge safe by instructing drivers to be careful,
instead of enforcing load limits and physical constraints.

---

## The Central Revelation

> **You cannot align a navigation system by shaping its stories.  
You must constrain where it can physically go.**

Safety is not a property of cognition.
It is a property of **reachable states**.
